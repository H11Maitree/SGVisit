Quazi Towsif ALI

As I reflect on my recent 5-day academic trip to Singapore, I can't help but feel grateful for the fantastic experiences I had there.

Upon arriving in Singapore on the first day, I was filled with excitement and anticipation for what was to come. The next day, I had the opportunity to visit the National University of Singapore (NUS) and attend a fascinating lecture. During lunch and the lab visit, I had the chance to network with fellow academics and see the impressive research facilities at NUS.

The third day was also packed with activities as I visited the office of Carousell, where I learned about e-commerce and start-ups in Singapore. I then went to the Singapore Management University (SMU) to learn from experts in my field.

After all the academic activities, I took a break on the fourth day and explored Singapore. I visited the Sentosa Island Aquarium, a great way to learn about marine life and have fun.

On the final day, I returned to Hong Kong feeling refreshed and inspired by my trip. Overall, it was an enriching experience that allowed me to learn from experts in my field, network with fellow academics, and explore a new place. I can't wait for my next academic trip!

- Quazi Towsif ALI

Mahir LABIB

My recent trip to Singapore was a wonderful experience that not only allowed me to explore the city's beauty but also helped me expand my professional network. I met many people from universities, industry professionals, and professors, which was a great learning experience.

I also had the opportunity to bond with new friends from the National University of Singapore (NUS) andthe Singapore Management University(SMU). We explored the city together and tried some of the local delicacies, which broadened my perspective and understanding of different cultures.

The city's transportation system was efficient, clean, and affordable, which made it easy for me to get around and explore different parts of the city. Additionally, the food was diverse, and I enjoyed exploring the different options available, from hawker centers to high-end restaurants.

Lastly, the people of Singapore were warm, friendly, and welcoming, and I learned a lot about their fascinating culture. Overall, it was an unforgettable trip that I will cherish for a long time.

- Mahir LABIB

Chi Wai YAU

In this 5-day tour visit, I learned a lot about how to collaborate with the others with different cultural backgrounds. This experience is very valuable since it is not easy to gather local and non-local students in an oversea trip.

Apart from the official activities, we have explored some famous sightseeing places in spare time such as Marina Bay Sand, Clarke Quay and Sentosa. Also, we have tasted various fresh durians in four popular durian stores which are in different residential areas. In the meanwhile, we have experienced their local culture such as taking transportation to non-tourist area, buying household goods from the local grocery store and talking with local residence in the restaurant.

Besides, I have learned how to use the professional software to design the props. During the trip, I have familiarized the photo taking and video shooting skills. After that, I have learned how to edit the video as well. This is a great chance to me to practice those useful multimedia editing techniques.

At last, I have Dr. Howard LEUNG and Dr. Kenneth LEE to thank for giving me the chance to participate in this memorable trip as well as enriching my wonderful University Life.

- Chi Wai YAU

Trung Hai LE

My recent excursion to Singapore proved to be a gratifying endeavor, as it not only afforded me the opportunity to immerse myself in the city's aesthetic allure but also expanded my perspective of technology and the field of computer science. I had the pleasure of meeting with a multitude of individuals hailing from esteemed educational institutions and multinational corporations.

The sharing of professors and students from the National University of Singapore (NUS) and the Singapore Management University (SMU) was engaging and helpful for prospective graduates. I particularly like the trip to Carousell where we were offered an insightful glimpse into the startup scene of Singapore and the working processes of an e-commerce company.

Outside of the scheduled activities, we traversed the city's expanse, partaking in the exploration of its manifold cultural treasures and indulging in the local delights. From the wilderness we witnessed atop Sentosa cable cars to the exquisite light and water show Spectra, this communal pursuit served to broaden my horizons and cultivate an understanding of how to blend nature and societal development.

I came back home still wanting to unravel more of the marvel that is Singapore. This odyssey will certainly remain etched in the recesses of my memory, evoking fond recollections for years to come.

—Trung Hai LE
